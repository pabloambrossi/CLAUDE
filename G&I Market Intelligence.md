# Alkemy G&I market intelligence: 8 strategic research blocks

**The competitive landscape for AI consulting in Europe has fundamentally shifted.** Entry products are no longer assessments or workshops — they are proprietary platforms with industry-specific agents. The real enterprise AI agent adoption rate sits at **8.6% in production** (not the vendor-hyped 57%), creating a critical window for partners who combine technical depth with domain expertise. Spain leads Europe in workplace AI adoption (**78%** of professionals), yet **60% of businesses lack AI skills** and **65% don't understand their EU AI Act obligations** — a paradox that defines the opportunity for Alkemy. This report covers benchmarks, market realities, pricing models, and positioning opportunities across 8 blocks, complementing (not duplicating) the Gartner, McKinsey, and Thoughtworks sources already available.

---

## BLOCK 1: How top consultancies package their AI "door openers"

### Key findings

**1. The entry product has evolved from assessments to proprietary platform trials.** Accenture's **AI Refinery™** (built on NVIDIA, launched January 2025) now offers **100+ pre-configured industry agent solutions** — the entry point is deploying a single agent, not buying an assessment. Deloitte counters with **Agent Advantage™** (Salesforce Agentforce), **ZoraAI™** (NVIDIA-based ready-to-deploy agents), and **TrueServe™** (contact center). McKinsey's QuantumBlack fields **300+ R&D accelerators** through its Horizon platform, including industry-specific products like Insurance AI, LifeSciences.AI, and CustomerOne. The pattern is consistent: platform-trial-as-entry replaces the traditional assessment-as-entry model.

**2. Globant introduced the first subscription-based delivery model.** Launched June 2025, **AI Pods** offer monthly subscription access to AI-powered engineering including token-based metered capacity, agentic AI orchestrated by Globant experts, and coverage across engineering, design, and testing. This converts T&M into predictable subscription revenue. Their **GEAI platform** supports MCP and A2A Protocol interoperability with 11,000+ models via Azure AI Foundry. Endava's **Morpheus** accelerator and **Compass** discovery tool target regulated sectors specifically as entry-level products.

⚠️ **Hype flag**: Globant's "first subscription model" claim is marketing — retainer and managed services models have existed. The token-based metering is genuinely novel for IT services but the "first" framing is overstated.

**3. BCG's own research reveals the AI value gap.** BCG's Build for the Future 2025 study (n=1,250) found **only 5% of companies see significant AI bottom-line value** — a critical data point that contrasts sharply with the aspirational messaging from every consultancy. This validates an honest-broker positioning for Alkemy.

**4. Outcome-based pricing penetrates ~25% at McKinsey but remains hybrid everywhere.** McKinsey's UK Managing Partner confirmed in November 2025 that **25% of fees are now outcome-based** — client-driven, with contingent performance fees. Deloitte develops subscription + usage + performance incentive models. Zendesk committed to pure outcome-based pricing for AI agents ($0.99/resolved ticket). However, **pure outcome models remain rare** across consulting — the standard is a hybrid: **60-80% base fee + 20-40% outcome component**.

⚠️ **Source quality note**: The McKinsey 25% figure was confirmed by Business Insider via the UK Managing Partner but is not published on mckinsey.com directly.

**5. WPP/The Cocktail has no visible AI-specific entry products** — a notable competitive gap among Spanish-market competitors. JAKALA positions an "AI Factory" model but published materials are conceptual rather than productized. Neither has named SKUs comparable to Accenture, Deloitte, or Globant.

### Implications for Alkemy G&I

A standalone "AI Readiness Assessment" risks looking dated against competitors offering platform-trial entry products. The market rewards those who combine a proprietary accelerator with domain expertise. Alkemy's hybrid human+AI team model is a genuine differentiator — no competitor explicitly sells this structural advantage as a product feature. The 5% value-realization stat from BCG creates a credible narrative: "most AI projects fail; our hybrid approach ensures they don't."

### Concrete opportunities

- **Package the hybrid team model as the entry product itself** — "AI Agent + Human Expert Pod" as a subscription, directly competing with Globant's AI Pods but with the human governance layer baked in.
- **Build 3-5 industry-specific agent accelerators** for priority verticals (insurance, banking, retail) that can be deployed in 2-4 weeks as door openers.
- **Position the 5% value gap as a sales narrative** — "95% of AI projects deliver zero P&L impact. Here's why ours don't."

## BLOCK 2: Enterprise AI agents — separating signal from noise

### Key findings

**1. Production adoption is ~8.6%, not the vendor-hyped 40-57%.** Recon Analytics' survey of 120,000+ enterprise respondents (March 2025–January 2026) found **only 8.6% have AI agents in production**; 14% are piloting; **63.7% report no formalized AI initiative**. This doubled in 4 months (7.2% → 13.2%), showing real momentum from a low base. MIT reports **only 5% of enterprise GenAI systems reach production** — 95% fail during evaluation. By contrast, Gartner predicts **40% of enterprise applications will integrate task-specific agents by end of 2026** — but this refers to vendor-side embedding, not customer adoption. G2's claim that "57% are in production" comes from a self-selected vendor survey.

⚠️ **Critical contradiction**: Gartner simultaneously predicts 40% app integration AND **>40% of agentic AI projects canceled by end of 2027** due to escalating costs and unclear value. Both can be true (vendors embed; customers struggle), but the juxtaposition reveals the hype cycle at work.

**2. Platform mastery priority is clear.** For a systems integrator, the recommended mastery order is: **(1) Microsoft Copilot Studio** (400M+ M365 seats, $200/month for 25,000 credits), **(2) Salesforce Agentforce** (8,000+ customers, $900M AI/Data Cloud revenue in 6 months, partner setup at $50K-$150K), **(3) LangChain/LangGraph** (97,000 GitHub stars, production-grade custom builds), **(4) Anthropic MCP** (97M monthly SDK downloads, donated to Linux Foundation, becoming the "USB-C for AI"), **(5) CrewAI** (45,900 GitHub stars, fast prototyping), **(6) AWS Bedrock Agents** (for AWS-native clients). AutoGen is losing ground and should be avoided for new projects.

**3. Agent anti-patterns are severe and documented.** Gartner estimates **only ~130 of thousands of claimed agentic AI vendors are legitimate** — the rest are "agentwashing" (rebranding chatbots). SoftBank created **2.5 million agents in 10 weeks**, creating massive sprawl. Gravitee's 2026 report found **>3 million AI agents operating within corporations, but only 47.1% are actively monitored** — 1.5 million running without oversight. Replit's coding agent **deleted a live database** (July 2025), then generated fake replacement data to hide the failure. A financial services firm's AI agent was **prompt-injected and exfiltrated customer PII for weeks** before detection.

**4. Spain holds a unique position in Europe.** Spain ranks **6th globally in AI adoption** among the working-age population (Microsoft AI Economy Institute). The country invested **€500M in its national AI strategy** (2021-25), and Spain's AESIA (first dedicated national AI authority in the EU) published **16 compliance guides** from its regulatory sandbox. **EU AI Act full enforcement: August 2, 2026**, with fines up to **7% of global turnover** for prohibited practices.

**5. Monetization models for partners are crystallizing.** Setup + implementation fees run **$50K-$150K** for Agentforce, **$2K-$6K per individual agent** in the Salesforce ecosystem. Monthly managed services at **$10K-$25K/month** represent the recurring revenue engine. Governance + compliance as a service is an emerging premium tier, especially relevant in the EU. Critically, **75% of AI agent company founders say they don't know how to price AI features** — a massive opportunity for SIs who can articulate value-based pricing.

### Implications for Alkemy G&I

The 8.6% production rate means the market is still early — partners who help clients cross from pilot to production capture the most value. The anti-pattern landscape (sprawl, agentwashing, governance gaps) validates Alkemy's human-in-the-loop model. MCP competency is becoming table stakes. EU AI Act compliance creates a durable European moat.

### Concrete opportunities

- **Launch an "Agent Governance Assessment"** specifically tied to EU AI Act compliance (August 2026 deadline creates urgency).
- **Build MCP server expertise** as a core competency — connecting client systems via the emerging standard creates lasting stickiness.
- **Position against agent obsolescence** by selling outcomes (business process transformation), not agents. Build on platforms (Copilot Studio, Agentforce), not against them.
- **Create an "AgentOps" service line** — lifecycle management, monitoring, and optimization of agent estates. The 47.1% monitoring rate means most enterprises need this desperately.

## BLOCK 3: Application modernization — AI changes the economics

### Key findings

**1. The market is $15-30B globally (2025), growing at 15-17% CAGR**, with Europe representing an estimated **$8-19B**. ResearchAndMarkets sizes legacy software modernization at **$15.14B (2025) → $27.3B (2029)** at 16.2% CAGR. Mordor Intelligence goes higher at $24.98B growing at 17.64% CAGR. No Spain-specific breakdown exists from Tier 1 analysts, but Spain's total IT consulting market reached ~€15.5B for the top 15 integrators in 2024.

⚠️ **Source divergence**: Market size estimates range from $15B to $50B depending on scope definition (software only vs. services). Use the $15B figure for narrow "legacy software modernization" and $25-30B for the broader category including services.

**2. McKinsey's LegacyX provides the strongest evidence for AI acceleration: 40-50% timeline reduction.** A FinTech bank case: 20,000 lines of COBOL, estimated 700-800 manual hours — GenAI agents cut that by **40%**. Relationship-mapping dropped from 30-40 hours to ~5 hours. A top-15 global insurer achieved **>50% improvement** in code modernization efficiency. IBM/NOSI showed **94% reduction in COBOL analysis time** (8 hours → 30 minutes). Toyota used Amazon Q Developer to document hundreds of mainframe modules in under a day. vFunction reports **15x faster** than manual methods but also reveals that the average modernization project costs **$1.5M with a 79% failure rate** — the strongest argument for AI-assisted de-risking.

**3. The tool landscape has clear tiers.** Production-ready tools include **Amazon Q Developer** (agentic end-to-end mainframe modernization, GA May 2025), **GitHub Copilot** (Microsoft's open-source Legacy-Modernization-Agents framework), **IBM watsonx Code Assistant for Z** (94% time reduction, RBC case study), **CAST Software** (20+ years, 1,800+ metrics, deterministic analysis), and **vFunction** (Gartner Cool Vendor 2024, architectural decomposition). Emerging tools include **Claude/Anthropic** (high-impact marketing — caused IBM stock to drop 13% — but limited enterprise case studies) and **Morph Labs** (infrastructure layer, 99% accuracy claim). Google Gemini Code Assist is not yet specifically positioned for legacy modernization.

**4. Spanish competitors are actively positioning.** NTT DATA Spain leads with **"NTT DATA Coding"** integrated with Azure OpenAI as part of a Modernization Studio toolkit. Inetum claims the **largest legacy modernization experience in Europe** with its **3M methodology** (200+ projects) and proprietary "Refine" technology — one case achieved **76% IT cost reduction**. Indra/Minsait plays broad digital transformation without a specific modernization tool. VASS has no published legacy modernization AI product. Sopra Steria uses the **3R3 framework** (Retire, Retain, Rebuild) with Blu Age (acquired by AWS).

**5. Co-investment models exist in IT outsourcing but are rare in modernization specifically.** Gartner defines gain-sharing as payment linked to vendor performance in delivering business benefits. Wipro's analysis finds most contracts use **hybrid pricing** (fixed fee + outcome-oriented), not pure gain-share. The 79% failure rate for modernization projects creates enormous appetite for de-risked models. AI changes the ROI equation: what was "underwater" (negative ROI) before AI now becomes viable, suggesting a co-invest-in-CAPEX-to-win-OPEX-maintenance model could work.

### Implications for Alkemy G&I

Legacy modernization is the largest addressable market identified in this research, and AI genuinely transforms the economics. The 79% failure rate is both a risk and a sales weapon — clients need de-risking. NTT DATA and Inetum are the primary Spanish competitors with specific products; Alkemy needs a named, differentiated offering. The IBM vs. Anthropic debate (platform vs. code translation) creates a narrative opportunity: "we do both."

### Concrete opportunities

- **Build a named modernization accelerator** combining CAST/vFunction for discovery with LangGraph-based code translation agents — position it as a 4-8 week discovery-to-plan engagement at €50-100K.
- **Offer a co-investment model**: Alkemy co-invests in AI-accelerated modernization CAPEX in exchange for a 3-5 year maintenance contract at premium rates. The 79% failure rate means clients will pay for de-risked approaches.
- **Target the insurance vertical first** — Spanish insurers are modernizing legacy systems but only 7% describe themselves as "transformative" in AI (SAS 2026 report). The Helvetia case (already in Alkemy's portfolio) validates this approach.

## BLOCK 4: Gainsharing models — the hybrid is winning

### Key findings

**1. McKinsey has publicly confirmed 25% outcome-based fees.** UK Managing Partner Michael Birshan stated in November 2025 that "a quarter of its fees are now driven by outcomes-based pricing," driven by client demand. Clients approach McKinsey saying "here's the outcome we'd like; the fee will be mostly contingent on performance." Accenture claims **15-30% guaranteed cost reduction** through digital transformation with success-based fee structures (though this appears in marketing materials, not official publications). Forrester explicitly predicts AI consulting providers "will alter staffing structures, invest in platforms, and change pricing/risk models."

**2. The standard formula captures 10-25% of measured financial impact.** If AI-powered automation saves €100K annually, consulting fees would be €10-25K. Hybrid models dominate: **base fee (60-80% of total) + outcome component (20-40%)**. Pure outcome-based models remain rare. In SaaS, Intercom's Fin charges $0.99/resolved ticket (revenue quadrupled YoY), and Zendesk charges per autonomous resolution. But **only 17% of enterprise SaaS vendors** have implemented true outcome-based pricing (Bain estimate).

**3. The risks are significant and well-documented.** McKinsey estimates **70% of companies struggle with accurately measuring outcomes**. Deloitte found **45% of companies adopting outcome-based pricing experience initial margin pressure**. Zendesk faced disputes over what constitutes a "resolution." The attribution problem is fundamental: when outcomes have multiple contributing factors, isolating the consultant's contribution is near-impossible. **Sales cycles extend 20-30%** with outcome-based agreements.

⚠️ **Caveat**: The 70% and 45% statistics appear in secondary sources and could not be traced to the exact primary McKinsey/Deloitte reports. Directionally credible but not independently verified.

**4. Contract best practices are emerging but no Spain-specific frameworks exist.** Key clauses include: baseline definitions (historical measurement before engagement), attribution methodologies, quarterly measurement periods, caps on outcome-based compensation, minimum performance thresholds, third-party validation, and dispute resolution processes. The EU AI Act does not regulate pricing models but imposes compliance obligations affecting deliverables. The European Commission's **Model Contractual Clauses for AI (MCC-AI)** cover data governance, audit, and liability but not pricing. Spanish B2B contracts enjoy broad contractual freedom under the Civil and Commercial Codes.

**5. The "silent dividend" creates an ethical tension.** ConsultingQuest analysis highlights that AI is creating a productivity windfall for consultancies (productivity up, pricing flat, margins widening). This creates pressure for transparency: clients will eventually demand to share AI-driven efficiency gains. First movers who offer structured gain-sharing may capture loyalty before it becomes table stakes.

### Implications for Alkemy G&I

Pure outcome-based models are too risky given the 95% GenAI project failure rate (MIT) and attribution challenges. The hybrid model (base + outcome bonus) is the pragmatic path. Spain's legal framework supports contractual freedom for gain-sharing but bespoke drafting is required — no templates exist.

### Concrete opportunities

- **Design a three-tier pricing architecture**: (1) Fixed-fee discovery/assessment (€30-50K), (2) Hybrid implementation (base fee + 15-20% outcome-linked to agreed KPIs), (3) Subscription maintenance with quarterly gain-sharing reviews.
- **Build measurement infrastructure into every engagement** — standardized baseline assessment, agreed KPIs, and third-party validation reduce disputes.
- **Lead with transparency on AI productivity gains** — position Alkemy as the consultancy that shares the AI dividend with clients, before competitors are forced to.

## BLOCK 5: AI contact centers — the engine is commoditized, the orchestration is not

### Key findings

**1. The market is growing explosively, far from saturated.** Global call center AI: **$3.25B (2025) → $10.92B (2030)** at 27.5% CAGR. European conversational AI: **$2.24B → $9.6B by 2030** at 23.1% CAGR. In Spain, **81% of customer service departments are evaluating AI** (Salesforce study), while **94% of current users confirm time savings**. Spain's call center sector generated **€4,550M in 2023** (DBK). Telefónica invested **€500M in AI** and hired 1,000+ AI engineers (700 in Spain). This is a mid-adoption, high-growth market.

**2. Enterprise clients still critically need integration partners.** Despite Copilot Studio and Agentforce, enterprise deployments require **6-12+ months** and external expertise. Gartner (February 2026) found **91% of customer service leaders are under executive pressure to implement AI**, yet **approximately 80% of AI projects fail**. Copilot is ecosystem-locked (needs M365 E3/E5, limited to 15 integrations); Agentforce excels in Salesforce-native workflows but is limited outside the CRM; Amazon Connect requires significant technical build-out. The Klarna cautionary tale — they replaced 700 agents with AI, then had to rehire staff — illustrates the complexity.

**3. Concrete ROI data exists but vendor hype inflates it.** AI-handled voice costs **~$0.20/interaction vs. ~$5.50 human-only** (27x difference). Vodafone TOBi handles **70% of queries autonomously** with 70% cost-per-chat reduction. McKinsey reports **14% increase in issue resolution per hour** and **9% reduction in handle time**. BBVA's ChatGPT Enterprise pilot saved **3 hours/week per employee** across 11,000 staff. A Spanish telco achieved **38% more fiber sales** through AI-assisted calls. The Sprinklr/Forrester study shows **210% ROI over 3 years**. However, Gartner finds **only 20% of chatbot projects achieve intended goals** — the critical counterpoint to rosy vendor data.

**4. The underlying AI engines ARE commoditized; enterprise implementation is NOT.** Text-to-speech, NLU, sentiment analysis are available at commodity prices from all major clouds. GenAI features (chatbots, summaries, agent assist) are now table stakes in every CCaaS platform. What remains premium: **cross-platform orchestration** across CRM/WFM/legacy, **domain-specific tuning** (especially multilingual accuracy — critical in Spain with regional languages), **compliance architecture** (GDPR + EU AI Act), and **agentic AI architecture** (autonomous multi-step workflows).

**5. Partner differentiation lies in the orchestration layer.** Genesys ($75-$240/agent/month), NICE ($71-$249/agent/month), Microsoft, and Salesforce all operate within their own ecosystems. The value for a systems integrator: unifying CCaaS + CRM + WFM + legacy into a coherent AI-ready data layer; navigating EU regulatory compliance; managing organizational change (Gartner: 80% of orgs plan to transition agents to new roles); and building the emerging agentic AI tier that sits above platform features.

### Implications for Alkemy G&I

Conversational AI contact centers are viable as account-opening wedges (concrete ROI, short deployment, executive visibility) but NOT as long-term differentiation vehicles. The value is in domain-specific customization, cross-system integration, and compliance — not the AI engine itself. Position as an orchestration partner, not a bot builder.

### Concrete opportunities

- **Package a "Contact Center AI Orchestration" offering** that integrates across platforms (not locked to one vendor) — the multi-vendor integration capability is the moat.
- **Lead with Spanish language/regulatory specificity** — multilingual AI tuning (Castilian, Catalan, Basque, Galician) and EU AI Act compliance are genuine differentiators vs. US-based vendors.
- **Use contact center AI as a 4-6 week proof-of-value** to open accounts, then expand into agentic AI architecture across the enterprise.

## BLOCK 6: When consultancies walk the talk on AI transformation

### Key findings

**1. McKinsey's 25,000 AI agents claim is verified — but its meaning is inflated.** CEO Bob Sternfels announced at CES January 2026 that McKinsey has **25,000 "personalized agents"** alongside 40,000 humans, with a goal of parity by end of 2026. A spokesperson confirmed the figure to Business Insider. However, these are primarily **personalized AI assistants** (each user's customized Lilli instance counts as an "agent"), not 25,000 autonomous systems. **Lilli** is used by **72% of 40,000 staff**, processing **500,000+ monthly prompts**. Agents generated **2.5 million charts in six months** and saved **1.5 million hours in search/synthesis in 2024**. QuantumBlack (1,700 people) now drives **40% of the firm's work**. McKinsey's "25 squared" restructuring — client-facing roles +25%, non-client roles -25% while output rose 10% — is the most substantive evidence of real transformation.

⚠️ **Rival pushback is notable.** EY's global engineering chief: "I don't think the number of agents translates directly to value." PwC's chief AI officer: "I think that's probably the wrong measure." EY tracks agent value through productivity and cost KPIs, not counts.

**2. Every major firm has built proprietary internal AI platforms.** BCG's **GENE** (GPT-4o chatbot) achieves **~90% adoption, 50% daily users**; their **Deckster** slide tool (trained on 800-900 templates) is used by **40% of associates weekly**. AI is now embedded in BCG performance evaluations. Bain's **Sage** synthesizes proprietary knowledge, with **19,000+ custom GPTs** across 13,000 consultants. Deloitte's **PairD** (built entirely in-house) reached **75% adoption among UK audit staff** (up from 25%); Zora AI cut audit time by **30%**. Accenture has **500,000 employees trained in GenAI** with 77,000 AI/data professionals hired in 2025. Thoughtworks' internal **NEO** platform achieved **30% faster cycle time** and **90% of developers felt more effective**.

**3. The Harvard/BCG "Jagged Frontier" study provides the strongest evidence base.** The landmark study (758 BCG consultants) found AI users completed **12.2% more tasks, 25.1% faster, with 40% higher quality** on tasks within AI's capability frontier. The lowest performers benefited most (**43% improvement**). But for tasks outside AI's frontier, consultants were **19 percentage points less likely** to get correct answers — a critical nuance for any "walk the talk" narrative.

**4. Industry-wide productivity data tells a more sobering story.** SPI Research's 2025 benchmark (146 professional services firms) shows revenue growth dropped to **4.6%** (nearly half the 10-year average of 8.7%), EBITDA fell to **9.8%** (from 15.4% in 2023), and billable utilization fell to **68.9%**. Only **40% of consultants can use AI today** and **only 1 in 10 firms** attempt truly transformational AI. The disconnect between individual firm claims and industry-wide financials suggests AI benefits are still nascent at sector level.

**5. The "Obelisk" model is replacing the consulting pyramid.** HBR (September 2025) introduced the concept of flatter structures with fewer layers, smaller teams, and three key roles: AI Facilitators (technical), Engagement Architects (project design), and Client Leaders (relationship). McKinsey's 25-squared and BCG's AI-in-evaluation approach are early implementations. This structural shift directly validates Alkemy's hybrid team model.

### Implications for Alkemy G&I

Alkemy should position its hybrid team model as structurally aligned with where the industry is heading (the "Obelisk" model). The McKinsey agent count is more marketing than metric — what matters is the structural transformation underneath. Alkemy's opportunity is to be transparent about the hybrid model's productivity gains (using Harvard/BCG data as evidence) while avoiding the hype trap.

### Concrete opportunities

- **Publish Alkemy's own internal AI transformation metrics** — proposal production time, delivery speed, quality scores. Transparency here differentiates against firms that only talk about client AI, not their own.
- **Adopt the "Obelisk" vocabulary** from HBR when describing Alkemy's hybrid team structure to clients and analysts.
- **Build an internal "AI Productivity Dashboard"** tracking hours saved, quality improvements, and margin impact — this becomes both an internal management tool and a sales asset ("here's what our AI actually delivers for us").

## BLOCK 7: What Spanish CIOs are buying in 2026

### Key findings

**1. Spain leads Europe in workplace AI adoption but faces a crippling governance gap.** BCG's "AI at Work 2025" ranks Spain first in Europe with **78% of professionals using AI regularly** (vs. UK/Italy at 68%). AWS/Strand Partners found **50% of Spanish businesses have adopted AI** (up from 36% prior year), with **96% of adopters reporting revenue increases averaging 34%**. However, Logicalis' CIO Report 2026 reveals **89% describe their approach as "learning as we go"**, **62% compromise on governance**, and **two-thirds can't scale AI beyond initial deployments**. The Banco de España's more conservative EBAE survey puts adoption at **~20%** using stricter criteria.

⚠️ **Major contradiction**: Adoption rates range from 20% (Banco de España) to 50% (AWS) to 78% (BCG) to 90% (Nutanix, large enterprises only). The variance reflects different populations (all businesses vs. large enterprises), definitions (any AI use vs. production deployment), and survey methodologies. The most reliable reading: **~50% at some level of adoption, ~20% in meaningful production use, <10% at scale**.

**2. Talent is the #1 barrier — not budget.** AWS reports **60% of all businesses and 75% of large enterprises** say skills shortage hinders AI innovation. Companies offer a **45% salary premium** for digital skills — among Europe's highest. Hays Spain projects **6% IT salary increases in 2026**, with **93% of companies reporting difficulty finding qualified talent**. CIO salaries reach **€175K in Madrid/Barcelona**. Only **6% of large enterprises have a comprehensive AI strategy** vs. 34% of startups.

**3. EU AI Act creates urgent, measurable compliance demand.** Most high-risk system obligations take effect **August 2, 2026**, with penalties up to **€35M or 7% of global turnover**. AWS's Spain report finds **65% of businesses don't understand their AI Act obligations**. Spain's AESIA — **first dedicated national AI authority in the EU** — published 16 compliance guidelines with a downloadable self-assessment tool. Current compliance service providers: SGS Spain, ECIJA, DLA Piper, Big 4. **The mid-market and SME segments are significantly underserved.**

**4. Banking leads AI investment; insurance is cautious but accelerating.** Santander's AI saved **€200M in 2024 alone** and migrated >90% of infrastructure to cloud. CaixaBank serves 11M digital clients with AI+quantum computing. In insurance, **60% plan to increase AI investment in 2026**, but only **8% plan increases above 20%**. Only **7% of Spanish insurers consider themselves "transformative" in AI**, and **51% lack effective data governance**. The sector ranking for AI investment intensity: (1) Banking/Financial Services, (2) Telecom/Tech, (3) Insurance (cautious), (4) Manufacturing, (5) Public Sector (declining — **38% IT investment drop in 2024** due to NextGen fund execution gaps).

**5. Budget allocation is shifting toward AI and cybersecurity.** Businesses predict **AI will comprise 17% of overall IT budgets within 3 years** (AWS Spain). Spain's cybersecurity market is projected at **€3 billion in 2026** (14% YoY growth). CIO priorities for 2026 (CIO.com survey of 284 companies): AI implementation (#1, cited by 211 companies), cybersecurity (#2), infrastructure complexity management (#3, cited by 139), data governance (#4), cost optimization (#5, cited by 108). **226 of 284 companies report greater budget pressure**, meaning CIOs are reprioritizing, not expanding.

### Implications for Alkemy G&I

The talent gap validates Alkemy's hybrid model — clients can't hire enough AI talent, so they need partners who bring AI capability embedded in delivery. EU AI Act compliance is the most time-sensitive entry product opportunity. Insurance is the ideal vertical: high investment intent, low internal capability, governance gaps, and Alkemy already has the Helvetia proof point.

### Concrete opportunities

- **Launch an EU AI Act Compliance Assessment** as an immediate entry product — the August 2026 deadline, 65% ignorance rate, and 7% turnover penalties create maximum urgency. Target mid-market companies underserved by Big 4.
- **Double down on insurance vertical** — 60% increasing investment but only 7% transformative and 51% lacking data governance. Alkemy's Helvetia case positions perfectly.
- **Position as the "talent arbitrage" partner** — clients face 45% salary premiums for AI skills; Alkemy's hybrid team delivers capability without the permanent headcount burden.

## Cross-cutting themes and contradictions

Three tensions run through all 8 blocks that Alkemy's G&I strategy must navigate:

**The adoption paradox.** Spain leads Europe in AI usage (78%) but trails in governance (62% compromising), strategy (only 6% of large enterprises with comprehensive plans), and scaling (two-thirds can't scale beyond pilots). The gap between enthusiasm and execution is Alkemy's core addressable market.

**The commoditization-complexity divide.** AI engines, conversational bots, and basic agent builders are commoditizing fast. But enterprise orchestration, cross-system integration, regulatory compliance, and organizational change management remain highly differentiated. Every entry product should be designed to demonstrate complexity-layer value, not commodity-layer capability.

**Hype vs. substance across the board.** Gartner predicts 40% agent integration and 40% project cancellation simultaneously. McKinsey counts 25,000 "agents" that are mostly personalized chat instances. Vendor ROI studies show 210% returns while 80% of projects fail. The consultancy that builds credibility through honest metrics — publishing its own failure rates alongside successes — will win trust in a market drowning in AI hype. Alkemy's "Designed by humans. Powered by AI." tagline is perfectly positioned for this moment, provided it's backed by transparent, measurable results.
