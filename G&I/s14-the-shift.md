# S14: The Shift -- From Capability Silos to Market-Driven Solutions

## VISUAL (what appears on screen)
- Title: "The Shift"
- Central transformation graphic -- five parallel arrows, each showing a FROM on the left and a TO on the right:
  1. Selling hours --> Selling results
  2. Capability silos --> Integrated solutions
  3. Fixed annual catalog --> Quarterly market adaptation
  4. Alkemy's internal language --> The client's language
  5. Individual practices --> Cross-practice delivery by default
- Below the arrows, a three-column block showing G&I's role in making this real:
  - (1) INNOVATE VALUE PROPOSITION -- Design what we sell
  - (2) BOOST SALES & CONVERSION -- GTM focus, content, ticket and conversion improvement
  - (3) GROW STRATEGIC CLIENTS -- Year 1 cross/upsell in key accounts
- Anchor line at bottom: "Same talent. New packaging. The machines are the WHAT. The practices are the HOW."
- So What: "We are not restructuring. We are repackaging how the market sees us -- and how we go to market."

## VERBAL (what the presenter says)
- This slide is the heart of what we are proposing. Everything before explains why. Everything after shows how. This is the what.
- Five shifts. Each one is simple to say, hard to execute.
- First: from selling hours to selling results. The market is done paying for time. They want outcomes.
- Second: from silos to integrated solutions. A client asking about AI visibility does not care if the answer comes from Media or Martech. They want the answer.
- Third: from a fixed catalog that changes once a year to quarterly adaptation. The market moves too fast. GEO did not exist as a service category 18 months ago. AAMP was published two weeks ago.
- Fourth: we stop using our internal language and start using theirs. Not "BX + Media + Martech" -- instead "Brand & Social Commerce, Search & AI Visibility, Conversion Intelligence."
- Fifth: cross-practice delivery becomes the default, not the exception. Helvetia proved this works.
- And G&I's role is clear: we design the value proposition, we improve how it gets sold, and we grow the key accounts in year one.
- Critical point: this is a PROPOSAL. It needs validation with each practice leader and GTM team. But the direction is grounded in what the market is telling us.
- The talent does not change. The capabilities do not change. What changes is the packaging and how we go to market.
