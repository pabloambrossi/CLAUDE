### Media (incl. GEO)

**THE BUSINESS REALITY:**
Media buying is becoming less profitable. Clients are internalizing their ad spend credit lines, and margins on media management are brutally low (~6-7% of investment). With our current client base (smaller accounts), we have two options:
1. **Grow volume** — unlikely, we're not on the radar of large advertisers
2. **Offer higher-margin differential services** — THIS IS THE PATH

The strategy: move from commodity media buying to high-value services that let us access Tier 2 / mid-market clients with better profitability. The team is strong — we just need to work smarter and offer what the market actually pays premium for.

**What we do today:**
- SEO/SEM management (Semrush, Screaming Frog)
- Programmatic buying (DV360, Adform, Stackadapt)
- Social ads management
- CTV buying (existing capability)
- DOOH, Audio Online, Branded Content
- Affiliation
- Retail Media (Witailer partnership in group, Reetmo)

**What the market demands — prioritized for MARGIN and DIFFERENTIATION:**

| Market need | Urgency | Feasibility | Margin potential | Priority |
|-------------|---------|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| **GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)** — "if the model doesn't know you, it won't choose you" | CRITICAL — must move fast, clients will figure it out soon | MEDIUM — Scrolling partnership, team needs training | VERY HIGH — consulting-level margins, not media margins | **P1** |
| **AAMP (Agentic Advertising Management Protocols)** — IAB Tech Lab standard, March 2026. First open standard for agents to buy/sell/manage advertising | HIGH — we need to be positioned here early | LOW-MEDIUM — standard is brand new, but studying and preparing is essential | VERY HIGH (future) — defines next era of programmatic | **P2** |
| **Total Search** (SEM+SEO+GEO+Social Search+Marketplaces unified) | HIGH — clients want one strategy across all search surfaces | HIGH — we already do SEM+SEO, add GEO+Social Search | HIGH — higher ticket, harder to commoditize | **P2** |
| **Attention-based media** (aCPM replacing viewability — IAB Spain defines this as the new metric) | MEDIUM — industry shift, differentiator in pitches | MEDIUM — measurement evolution | MEDIUM — pitch differentiator more than revenue driver | **P3** |
| **AI-optimized media operations** (automated optimization, predictive allocation) | MEDIUM — efficiency play powered by Martech engine | MEDIUM — depends on Plan Iniciativas IA | HIGH — efficiency gains that improve our margins | **P3** |

**Note on Total Search:** This is the integration of ALL search channels (SEM + SEO + GEO + Social Search in TikTok/IG/YouTube + Marketplace search in Amazon/etc.) into a single, unified search strategy. Instead of managing each channel separately, we provide a holistic view of how the brand appears across ALL surfaces where people search — including AI-generated responses (GEO).

**Note on Attention-based media:** IAB Spain defines attention (measured as aCPM — attention-cost-per-mille) as the successor to viewability. Instead of paying for whether an ad was technically "viewable," advertisers pay for actual seconds of human attention. This shift favors quality placements over volume — which is exactly what a boutique agency like Alkemy should optimize for.

**Bridges needed:**
- Scrolling partnership activation ASAP (GEO training + joint delivery)
- Search Social Strategist hire (hybrid SEO+Social+GEO profile)
- AAMP study group — dedicate time to understand the standard and prepare
- Martech integration for attribution (MTA/MMM) to prove media ROI with data

## DATA.TECH.AI — THE RADICAL REPOSITIONING

### The existential question clients are asking
"Why should I hire you when I already have Claude/GPT? I can now do things that used to require experts."

This is real. And it's the single biggest threat AND opportunity for DTC. The honest answer:

**Yes, clients CAN use AI tools directly. But they CANNOT:**
- Build a proprietary, scalable, secure, modular ecosystem around those tools
- Align AI capabilities with their specific business objectives
- Orchestrate agents across their systems, data, and processes
- Ensure compliance with EU AI Act and DORA
- Maintain a continuously updated technology radar
- Turn experiments into production systems that deliver measurable results

**DTC's new role: The AI Enablement Partner**

We don't build FOR clients anymore. We enable clients to build WITH AI in an ecosystem that is:
- **Proprietary** — theirs, not ours. They own it.
- **Scalable** — grows with their ambition
- **Secure** — governance, compliance, guardrails built in
- **Modular** — components that can be swapped, upgraded, extended
- **Business-oriented** — every initiative tied to concrete business results

**What we provide as a partner (6 enablement layers):**

| Layer | What it means | Why clients can't do it alone |
|-------|--------------|------------------------------|
| **1. Data** | Foundation + governance + quality + accessibility | Their data is scattered, unstructured, ungoverned. AI without good data = garbage. |
| **2. Infrastructure** | Cloud, integrations, APIs, security architecture | They need enterprise-grade infrastructure, not a laptop with ChatGPT. |
| **3. Processes** | Workflows, automation, operational redesign | AI doesn't fit into old processes. Processes need redesigning around AI. |
| **4. Orchestration** | Agents, systems connected, MCP, A2A protocols | The hard part isn't building one agent — it's making 20 agents work together safely. |
| **5. Training** | Capability building for internal teams | They need to understand what they're using. Art. 4 AI Act makes this legally mandatory. |
| **6. Technology radar** | Continuous monitoring of what's new, what works, what to adopt | The landscape changes weekly. They can't track it AND run their business. |

**Everything oriented to CONCRETE RESULTS aligned with BUSINESS OBJECTIVES.**
Not "we deployed AI." But "we increased conversion by 18%" or "we reduced time-to-market by 40%."
